## Problem

White-space targets hide beyond obvious broker flow. Zurich can find more companies, but the business risk is flooding distribution and underwriting teams with weak opportunities that do not respect appetite, broker ownership, relationship timing, or current account context.

## Prototype

Zurich Scout creates validated account-review briefs, not quote-ready outreach. Each brief explains:

- why this account,
- why now,
- which Zurich lines may fit,
- who may influence the conversation,
- what could be wrong,
- and what Zurich must validate internally before anyone acts.

A `pursue` recommendation means "worth Zurich internal clearance now," not "ready to quote" and not "ready for automated outreach."

## Proof

The current run is a focused FDIC Financial Institutions pilot, not a full D&B 30,000-company run. It reviewed 70 accounts, produced 18 active validation candidates, deferred or rejected 52 lower-readiness accounts, mapped 304 public buyer / influencer paths, and passed schema/artifact validation with 0 issues.

## Business Value

The case points to a greater than $200M topline GWP growth opportunity from expanding target coverage. Zurich Scout makes that expansion operational by helping Zurich screen broad account universes, deep-research a smaller qualified set, and route the strongest accounts into broker-safe, underwriter-aware review.

The immediate value is focus:

- focus distribution and underwriting attention,
- respect broker ownership before outreach,
- convert public signals into CRM-ready account reviews.

Conservative focus model: if a first-pass SME review takes 30-60 minutes, deferring or rejecting 52 lower-readiness accounts avoids roughly 26-52 hours of low-readiness review time.

## Next Steps For Scaling

1. Run Zurich Scout on the Zurich-provided D&B 30,000-company universe.
2. Add Zurich internal overlay fields: existing customer, prior quote, broker owner, renewal date, loss-history flag, relationship owner, and appetite class.
3. Review the top 25 accounts with distribution and underwriting SMEs.
4. Measure internal-clearance pass rate, SME acceptance rate, broker conversations created, qualified account reviews, and false-positive reasons.
5. After Zurich confirms ownership and broker context, expand from qualification into relationship-led activation: warm-intro suggestions, broker asks, CRM tasks, and guarded outreach support.
